Think about arepas as a blank canvas made with corn, water, and sabor!('taste' in Spanish)

Arepas can be paired with any other savory food. Try the traditional Colombian arepa with cheese, or arepa with egg. If you feel bold, you can try the more complex Venezualan arepas, with shredded beef, beans, plantain and cheese (just to name a few). Think of arepas as an improved, gluten-free version of bread... yes, arepas are THAT "best thing since slice bread" that people are talking about.

Today I will teach you how to make the perfect arepa and I'll give you a few examples of what to fill them with

Ingredients

  • 2 cups corn meal
  • 2 ½ water
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 1 tbsp melted butter

Steps

  1. Pour water and melted butter on a mixing bowl
  2. Mix corn meal and salt
  3. Slowly incorporate the dry ingredients into water and butter mixture
  4. Mix together with a spoon until you have a homogeneous dough
  5. Let the dough rest for 5 minutes
  6. Divide the dough and form individual 2 inch spheres
  7. Use either a tortilla press or the palms of your hand to flatten the spheres into disks with roughly ½ inch thickness
  8. Slightly oil a large non-stick pan and cook the disks on medium heat for 5-7 minutes per side
  9. Optional: split the cooked arepa and fill it with cheese, beef, chiken, or anything else you want
